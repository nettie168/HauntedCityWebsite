New Skaters

New Skaters

We run new intake sessions to anyone who wants to learn to skate and play roller derby.

New intakes start every January and June

Open to anyone 18+ of any gender and ability.

No skating experience required

The Intake

The new intake runs for 15 weeks, where we safely teach you how to play roller derby.

The Intake will teach you how to:

  • Get up on skates and how to fall safely
  • Skate forwards and how to stop
  • Skate on one leg
  • Skate backwards
  • Do T-stops, crossovers, transitions and toe-stop runs

As well as the rules of roller derby, and the techniques like blocking, jamming and bracing.

We encourage you to attend as many sessions as you can, as many of the skills used in the later sessions are built on earlier ones.

The current intake began on 19th June 2024 and the next one starts in January 2025!

The first two taster sessions are £7. After that it is £20 a month plus £10 if you are hiring kit from us.

Spaces are limited, so contact us to book on.

All Genders Welcome

Whoa hold on a second, you’re a women’s team but I don’t identify as a woman.... How can I be involved?

The new skater sessions are open to all genders.

We have a competitive team, the Wraiths, that corresponds with the WFTDA (Women’s Flat Track Derby Association) gender statement that includes female identifying and non binary skaters.

And we have a recreational team for all skaters, including male identifying skaters, the Phantoms (referred to as OTA (open to all)).

Contact drills will be run in WFTDA and OTA groups. This is to ensure all participants feel comfortable being involved in skating at all levels within the gender statement rules of WFTDA Gender Policy and there will always be alternating options for both OTA and WFTDA

What happens next?

We hope you enjoy your intake, and want to carry on with roller derby.

Since we only have a limited amount of kit to rent out, we hope that all new skaters can now go out with their new found knowledge of roller derby and invest in their own kit, so that we can rent it to the new intake.

We do have a financial hardship policy, so if you are struggling, and can't get hold of your own kit, we might be able to let you rent the kit for longer. Just let us know as soon as you can.

Passing Red and Orange Skills

When you first join Haunted City you'll be going to our Sunday sessions, and be asked to pay monthly dues of £20, which go towards renting the hall, and other costs.

Depending on how fast you progress, you may be invited to the Thursdays sessions where you'll be allowed to practice and play in full competetive james, with the dues becoming £30 a month.
